Vector Components

An angled vector can be separated into simpler horizontal and vertical parts. These components allow us to calculate with direction using ordinary numbers.

Prerequisite: vectors Core idea: resolve into perpendicular parts Next: unit vectors

Lesson contents

  1. What components mean
  2. Resolving a vector
  3. Rebuilding a vector
  4. Check your understanding

Section 1

Components describe one vector in two directions

A vector that points diagonally can be understood as the combined effect of one horizontal vector and one vertical vector.

Vector components: perpendicular vectors that combine to produce the original vector.

In two dimensions, we usually choose a horizontal x-component and a vertical y-component. The original vector is not being replaced by two unrelated vectors. The two components are a different description of the same vector.

Original vector

  • One magnitude
  • One overall direction
  • Often drawn diagonally

Component form

  • Horizontal part
  • Vertical part
  • Together produce the original vector

Worked example: reading components

A displacement has an x-component of 4 meters east and a y-component of 3 meters north.

The object has moved both east and north. The combined displacement points northeast.

Section summary

Components separate a vector into perpendicular directions while preserving the original vector.

Section 2

Resolving a vector into components

To resolve a vector means to determine the horizontal and vertical vectors that combine to form it.

When a vector of magnitude V makes an angle θ above the positive horizontal direction, its components are:

Vx = V cos(θ) Vy = V sin(θ)

The cosine gives the horizontal share of the vector, while the sine gives the vertical share. Signs indicate direction: right and up are commonly positive; left and down are commonly negative.

Worked example: resolve a vector

A force of 10 newtons acts at 30° above the horizontal.

Fx = 10 cos(30°) ≈ 8.66 N Fy = 10 sin(30°) = 5.00 N

The force is equivalent to a horizontal component of approximately 8.66 newtons and a vertical component of 5.00 newtons.

Section summary

Use cosine for the component adjacent to the angle and sine for the component opposite the angle.

Section 3

Rebuilding a vector from its components

If the horizontal and vertical components are known, the original magnitude and direction can be recovered.

Magnitude

Because the components form a right triangle, the Pythagorean theorem gives the vector magnitude.

V = √(Vx² + Vy²)

Direction

The angle can be found from the ratio of the vertical component to the horizontal component.

θ = tan⁻¹(Vy / Vx)

Quadrant matters

A calculator may return an angle that does not identify the correct quadrant by itself. The signs of Vx and Vy must also be checked to determine the actual direction.

Worked example: reconstruct a vector

A displacement has components Vx = 6 meters and Vy = 8 meters.

V = √(6² + 8²) = √100 = 10 m θ = tan⁻¹(8 / 6) ≈ 53.1°

The displacement has a magnitude of 10 meters and points approximately 53.1° above the positive horizontal direction.

Section summary

Components and vector form contain the same information. Either description can be reconstructed from the other.

Section 4

Check your understanding

Before moving to unit vectors, make sure you can interpret, calculate, and reconstruct vector components.

Quick check

  1. What do the x- and y-components of a vector represent?
  2. A 12-newton vector acts at 0°. What are its components?
  3. A 12-newton vector acts at 90°. What are its components?
  4. Find the magnitude of a vector with components 5 and 12.
  5. Why must the signs of the components be checked when finding direction?
Show answers
  1. The perpendicular horizontal and vertical parts that combine to form the vector.
  2. Vx = 12 N and Vy = 0 N.
  3. Vx = 0 N and Vy = 12 N.
  4. 13 units.
  5. Because inverse tangent alone may return an angle in the wrong quadrant.

Lesson summary

  • A two-dimensional vector can be described by horizontal and vertical components.
  • For an angle measured from the positive horizontal axis, Vx = V cos(θ) and Vy = V sin(θ).
  • Component signs indicate direction.
  • The original magnitude is found with the Pythagorean theorem.
  • The original direction is found with inverse tangent plus a quadrant check.
