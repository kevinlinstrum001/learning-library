Proposed 12-Volt Yard Solar Power Setup

A small, expandable off-grid system centered on one 200-watt rigid solar panel, a 30-amp MPPT controller, a 12.8-volt 100Ah LiFePO4 battery, and a 700-watt pure sine-wave inverter.

● Designed for modest loads and learning—not for powering the entire house.
Concept Power flow Equipment Wiring Energy budget Build sequence Safety

Purpose and boundaries

System concept

This prototype is intended for learning, backup power, lighting, charging electronics, communications equipment, and other modest loads. It is deliberately small enough to understand and test, while leaving a reasonable path for later expansion.

Two paths define the system

1
Charging path
Sunlight → 200W solar panel → 30A MPPT controller → 12V 100Ah battery.
2
Load path
Battery → fuse and disconnect → 700W inverter → small 120V loads.

Critical inverter connection rule

The inverter connects directly to the battery through its own correctly sized fuse, short heavy cables, and a DC-rated disconnect. It does not connect to the charge controller’s small “load” terminals.

  • Not a whole-house power system.
  • Not a grid-tied installation.
  • Not a substitute for code-compliant household wiring.

Circuit protection

Protection and wiring plan

Final conductor sizes and fuse ratings depend on cable length, conductor temperature rating, installation method, connected equipment, and the current manufacturer instructions. Treat the following as a planning framework rather than a final construction schedule.

ConnectionProtection and materialsPlanning note
Panel → controllerUV-resistant PV wire, weather-rated connectors, cable support, and a PV disconnect.Maintain polarity and remain below the controller maximum PV voltage.
Controller → batteryShort copper conductors with a battery-side fuse or breaker sized for the controller circuit.The controller circuit requires external protection.
Battery → inverterVery short heavy copper cables, a main fuse close to battery positive, and a DC-rated disconnect.This is the highest-current branch in the system.
Battery monitorPlace the shunt in the battery negative path so all charging and load current passes through it.Only the battery side of the shunt should connect directly to battery negative.
High-current warning: at 700W output, the inverter may draw roughly 60A or more from a 12V battery after losses. Follow the inverter manual for cable and fuse sizing.

Build sequence

1 — Measure
Measure panel-to-controller and battery-to-inverter cable lengths before selecting wire and protection.
2 — Mount
Install and anchor the panel frame while avoiding shade, impact, standing water, and accidental contact.
3 — Assemble
Mount the controller, battery, fuses, disconnects, monitor, and inverter on a durable surface or in a suitable enclosure.
4 — Connect
With PV isolated, connect the battery to the controller first, configure battery type, and then connect the panel. Confirm the exact sequence in the current manual.
5 — Test
Verify polarity, charging, terminal tightness, voltage, monitor readings, and temperature before applying larger loads.

Non-negotiable limits

Safety boundaries

A 12V battery can deliver very high fault current. Good protection, short cable runs, correct hardware, and careful verification are essential even in a small system.

!
Do not connect the inverter to house wiring or an existing receptacle through a backfeed cord.
!
Use only DC-rated fuses, breakers, switches, connectors, and disconnects at the required voltage and current.
!
Place the main battery fuse close to the positive terminal.
!
Protect each conductor according to its ampacity and connected equipment.
!
Keep the battery within its permitted charging temperature range.
!
Confirm grounding, bonding, outdoor wiring, trenching, structural mounting, and local code requirements with a qualified installer or authority.
!
Recheck terminal torque and inspect periodically for heat, corrosion, abrasion, looseness, and water entry.
